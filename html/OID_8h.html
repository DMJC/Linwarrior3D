typedef unsigned long long OID

Serial-Code or OID for cObject (used for values, too).

Use whereever possible to store object references. Object A having a pointer to cObject B is like saying A and B are bound together. Pointers are ok between a Mech and its own weapon but not between a targeting weapon (or Mech) and a hostile target Mech. Only bind together what belongs together and what would be destroyed together. Pointers express responsibility whereas OIDs loosely couple. Use World::getInstance()->getObject(oid) to map serial to pointer.
